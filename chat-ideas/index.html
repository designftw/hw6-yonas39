Need Finding for Chat App

Interview Questions

  1. What is your preferred mode of communication (text, voice, video) and how does that affect your choice of messaging app?
  2. Can you describe any messaging app user interfaces that you find intuitive or confusing?
  3. Can you describe a situation where you needed to use a messaging app, and what were some of the challenges you faced?
  4. Have you ever experienced miscommunication or misunderstandings in a messaging app? If so, can you describe the situation and how it could have been avoided?
  5. What are some of the most important features you look for in a messaging app?
  6. How do you typically manage your conversations and keep track of important information in your messaging app?
  7. What is your preferred notification method for new messages in a messaging app? Do you have any specific customization preferences for notifications?
  8. Do you want messaging apps to have inbuild language options?

Interviewing and Key Insights

The list below is a detailed summary of the interviews I conducted and the keyinsights.
  1. Preferred mode of communication: All three participants preferred text over voice or video. They found it to be more convenient and less intrusive. These influenced their messaging app choice. They looked for apps with a good texting interface and features like read receipts, typing indicators, and message search.
  2. Intuitive user interfaces: Participants found messaging apps with simple and clean user interfaces to be the most intuitive. They appreciated features such as the ability to customize chat backgrounds, font size, and color schemes. They found messaging apps that had cluttered or confusing interfaces to be frustrating to use.
  3. Challenges faced while using messaging apps: Participants mentioned that one of their biggest challenges was managing multiple conversations and keeping track of important information such as dates, times, and locations. They also said finding specific messages or conversations in the app could be difficult.
  4. Miscommunication or misunderstandings: Two out of three participants had experienced miscommunication or misunderstandings in messaging apps. One participant had sent a message that was misinterpreted by the recipient, while another had received an unclear message. They suggested that better communication features such as voice or video calls, emojis, or more precise language could have avoided these situations.
  5. Essential features in messaging apps: Participants mentioned that the most critical elements in a messaging app were text formatting options, the ability to send images, videos, and documents, group chat options, message search, and customizable notification settings.
  6. Conversation management: Participants mentioned that they typically managed their conversations by categorizing them into different folders or using labels or tags. They also used features such as an archive or deleted to keep their chat list organized.
  7. Notification preferences: All participants preferred to receive push notifications for new messages, but with customizable settings such as the ability to turn off notifications for specific chats or during certain times of the day.
  8. In-built language options: All participants mentioned they would appreciate messaging apps with built-in language options. They suggested that this would be particularly useful for users who communicate with people from different regions or countries.
Based on these findings, a messaging app should prioritize features such as text formatting options, the ability to send images, videos, and documents, group chat options, message search, and customizable notification settings. The app should also have a clean and intuitive user interface that is easy to navigate. Additional features, such as categorizing conversations, labels, or tags, are also important. Finally, the app should have customizable language options to cater to a global audience.
